# Drift Marketing Playbook
## Based on the Cal AI growth framework by Jake Castillo — adapted for Drift

---

## What Drift Is (Know This Before Everything Else)

Drift is a **social travel discovery + AI trip planning app**.

- Users discover curated trips and itineraries from creators and the community
- AI generates full personalized itineraries from a single sentence prompt
- Users "steal" and remix other creators' trips
- Social travel profiles track countries, cities, and travel score
- Premium at $8.99/mo — not a booking app, a planning and discovery platform

**Tagline: "Your Passion Passport. Discover trips. Steal itineraries. Plan with friends."**

The Cal AI parallel: Cal AI was a calorie-tracking app that won by owning fitness content creators. Drift wins by owning travel planning creators — people who already make "how I planned my X trip" content organically.

## 1. Know What You're Actually Buying

### Three channels, three different purchases:

| Channel | What You Buy | Best For Drift |
|---|---|---|
| UGC | Content + algorithm swings | Volume testing, cheap hooks |
| Paid Ads (Meta/TikTok) | Predictable distribution | Scaling proven creative |
| Influencers | Audience trust | Primary growth engine |

Drift's core advantage mirrors Cal AI's: **travel creators already make the content you need.** A travel creator who shares itineraries is doing unpaid Drift marketing right now. You're just putting the product in the frame.

Influencer marketing works for Drift because:
- The travel niche embraces "how I planned my trip" and "steal my itinerary" formats that go viral naturally
- Mid-tier travel creators are abundant and affordable — everyone wants to be a travel creator
- Drift feels like a product these creators would genuinely use (it generates their content FOR them)
- The "steal my itinerary" feature is a natural call-to-action that converts

## 2. The 20-Second Test for Drift Creators

Three questions. If any answer is no, move on.

**Question 1: What do their views look like?**
A creator with 80K followers averaging 400K views per video beats one with 500K followers averaging 20K views. You're paying for the video, not the bio number.

**Question 2: What does the comment section look like?**
For Drift specifically, look for:
- "Where is this??" / "I need to go here"
- "Can you share your full itinerary?"
- "Saving this for my trip"
- "How did you find this??"

These comments signal an audience that actively plans trips — the exact user Drift converts.

**Question 3: Could you imagine being friends with this person?**
Does the camera feel like a window into their actual travel life, or a highlight reel from a stranger? The parasocial relationship is everything. If their audience feels like they know this person's travel style, Drift becomes a tool their friend uses — not an ad they scrolled past.

## 3. Creator Niches — Green List vs. Red List

### Green List (Test These First)

**1. Solo Female Travelers** ⭐ Highest Priority
- Audience: 18-35F, aspirational, planning-oriented, high trust in creator recommendations
- Content fit: "I planned my solo Italy trip using Drift" is natural and authentic
- The Cal AI equivalent: fitness girls who share their workout tracking — same trust dynamic
- Example profiles to target: solo travel, Europe trips, "what I packed", city guides from female perspective

**2. Itinerary Content Creators** ⭐ Highest Priority
- These people already make your product for you. A creator who posts "My 7-Day Japan Itinerary" is doing unpaid Drift content.
- The handoff is effortless: "I actually build all my itineraries in Drift — here's how"
- Look for creators whose entire channel is destination guides with day-by-day breakdowns

**3. Hidden Gems / Off-the-Beaten-Path Travel**
- Drift has a "Hidden Gems" tab built into the Explore screen — perfect content alignment
- These audiences are highly engaged and save/share content more than any other travel niche
- Viral format: "Nobody talks about [destination] but it's insane" — Drift is the discovery engine

**4. Digital Nomads / Remote Work Travel**
- Tech-forward audience already comfortable paying for productivity apps
- Travel frequency is high — they plan trips constantly, $8.99/mo subscription makes sense
- Content angle: "How I plan 3+ trips a month with Drift"

**5. Travel Couples (Adventure Budget Tier)**
- Not luxury mega couples — budget or mid-range adventure couples with 50K-300K followers
- High authenticity, strong comment sections, extremely loyal audiences
- Content angle: "We used Drift to plan our Portugal road trip and found this gem"

**6. "Dream Trip" Bucket List Creators**
- People who make content about places they want to visit, not just places they've been
- Drift's AI planner is the perfect product: describe your dream trip, get a full itinerary
- Hook: "I've always wanted to go to Japan but never knew where to start — I typed one sentence into Drift"

---

### Red List (Avoid These — Apply the Cal AI Lesson)

**Mega luxury travel accounts (5M+ followers):**
Audience watches to fantasize, not to plan. They don't book Drift-level trips. You're hiring an audience that doesn't convert.

**Travel deal / points / miles creators:**
Their audience is hyper price-sensitive and rewards-focused. Drift's value isn't in finding cheap flights — wrong product fit, wrong audience mindset.

**Celebrity travel content (athletes, musicians posting vacations):**
Same issue as Cal AI's UFC fighters. The audience watching Drake's Maldives vacation doesn't want to plan a trip — they're watching for the celebrity, not the destination.

**Pure videography / cinematography travel:**
Beautiful footage, zero parasocial relationship. No trust = no conversion. The audience follows for the art, not the person.

**Family travel with young children:**
Different life stage entirely. The product is built for solo/couple/friend group travel planners. The family travel audience is planning Disney trips, not using AI to discover hidden gems in Bali.

**Comedy creators who happen to travel:**
Cal AI's exact mistake. Huge reach but different audience every viral video. No consistent travel identity = no trust transfer to a travel planning app.

## 4. Pricing — The Middle of the Curve Is Where the Money Is

### The broken ends:
- **Top end (1M+ followers):** Overpriced because brands anchor to Meta CPMs without attribution. Walk away.
- **Bottom end (<5K followers):** Often quoted $500-1,500 for posts that get 2K views. Doesn't math.

### The Drift sweet spot: **30K–300K followers / 50K–500K average views**

| Creator Tier | Followers | Flat Rate Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 5K–15K | $75–$200 | Testing only, not scale |
| Micro | 15K–50K | $200–$600 | Good for niche testing |
| **Mid** | **50K–300K** | **$600–$2,500** | **Primary tier — scale here** |
| Macro | 300K–1M | $2,500–$6,000 | Only if strong view/comment metrics |
| Mega | 1M+ | Skip | Overpriced, can't attribute |

**Non-negotiable: always pay flat rates.** Never per-view, never rev share on installs for organic posts. The moment a video goes viral, your flat-rate deal becomes the best investment you've made. Per-view pricing converts organic reach into expensive Meta ads.

## 5. The Drift Creator Brief — Keep It Under 100 Words

Most brands kill influencer programs with 10-page decks and approval chains. Let creators cook.

### The Drift Brief Template:

```
Hey [Name] — here's everything you need:

Drift is a travel app where you discover trips from other creators, 
steal their itineraries, and use AI to plan your own. 
Describe your dream trip in one sentence — it builds the whole thing.

One thing you can't say: "book your trip through Drift" (we don't do booking).

Everything else? Make it feel like you. 
Your audience follows you for your travel taste — show them how Drift fits into that.
```

That's it. Send that doc. Get out of the way.

## 6. The 5 Ad Hooks That Will Convert for Drift

These are minimal content briefs — starting points for creators, not scripts.

### Hook 1: The AI Reveal
> "I described my dream trip to Japan in one sentence. This is what Drift built me. 😳"

*Shows the AI planner typing one sentence → full 7-day itinerary appearing*
Works because: demonstrates the core product magic in 10 seconds. Satisfies curiosity immediately.

---

### Hook 2: The Steal
> "Steal [creator name]'s exact Bali itinerary for free 👇"

*Shows the "steal itinerary" feature, discovering a creator's saved trip, remixing it*
Works because: "steal" is a dopamine hit in the headline. The social discovery feature is differentiated.

---

### Hook 3: The Time Comparison
> "Planning my last trip took me 3 weeks. This one took 30 seconds."

*Before: scrolling through Google docs, Pinterest boards, Reddit threads. After: one Drift prompt.*
Works because: relatable pain point for any traveler. Positions Drift as the obvious solution.

---

### Hook 4: The Hidden Gem
> "I found this place in [destination] through Drift. I've never seen it on anyone else's feed."

*Shows Drift's Hidden Gems explore tab, scrolling to discover a creator's off-the-beaten-path trip*
Works because: discovery content is the highest-save format on TikTok/Reels. Exploits FOMO directly.

---

### Hook 5: The Friend Flex
> "My friend planned the most insane trip to Portugal and she got the whole itinerary from this app"

*Third-person framing reduces ad detection. Feels like organic word-of-mouth.*
Works because: the parasocial dynamic extends — if the creator's friend trusts it, the audience does too.

## 7. Niche Testing — The 3-5 Rule for Drift

Test a niche 3-5 times with different creators and different hook angles before declaring it dead or scaling it.

### Phase 1 Test Matrix (First 90 Days)

| Niche | Test Budget | Creators | Kill Signal | Scale Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Solo female travel | $3,000 | 3-4 mid-tier | <20K views avg + dead comments | >100K views + "how do I get this app?" comments |
| Itinerary creators | $2,500 | 3-4 mid-tier | Low saves, no comment engagement | High saves + "what app is this?" |
| Hidden gems | $2,000 | 3-4 micro | <15K views avg | Saves > 2% of views |
| Digital nomads | $2,000 | 3 micro | Low CTR to app store | Strong comment intent signals |
| Budget travel couples | $2,500 | 3-4 mid-tier | <15K views + emoji-only comments | Authentic Q&A in comments |

**Total Phase 1 budget: ~$12,000 across 15-20 deals**

At 3-5 creators per niche, you'll know within 60-90 days which niches convert. Double down on 2 winners, kill 3 losers, re-invest into scale.

## 8. Creator System — How to Manage 100+ Partnerships with 4 People

The Cal AI system adapted for Drift:

**Step 1: Volume Outreach**
VAs running outreach via TikTok DMs, Instagram DMs, and creator marketplaces (Creator.co, AspireIQ, Collabstr). Target: 50+ outreach per niche per week. Use templated but personalized DMs that reference a specific video of theirs.

**Step 2: Instant Calls**
When a creator responds positively, jump on a 15-minute call same day. By end of call: yes or no. Not "let me check with my team." You: yes, rate, deliverables, timeline. Done.

**Step 3: One-Click Contracts**
Yes on the call triggers: contract sent (Docusign), flat fee, post count, deliverable specs (60s TikTok, 30s Reels), post date, payment terms. No lawyer back-and-forth.

**Step 4: Master Tracker**
Notion or Airtable: Creator name → Platform → Followers → Avg Views → Rate → Contract Date → Post Date → Deliverables → Views Logged → Renew Y/N.
Signed contract auto-updates the tracker.

**Step 5: Automated Post-Day Reminder**
Text/email automation: "Hey [Name], today's your scheduled post day! Submit your video for review by [time]." Zapier/Make handles this automatically.

**Step 6: Track and Renew**
Post goes live → log views at 48h and 7 days → log comment quality (actual conversation vs emoji spam) → automatic renewal score. Renew anyone above 50K avg views with genuine engagement. Cut the rest.

## 9. Paid Ads — Scaling Proven Organic Creative

Once influencer content is tested and winning videos are identified, repurpose them as paid ads.

**The Drift paid ads funnel:**
1. Organic influencer post goes live → track views organically
2. Videos hitting 100K+ views → get whitelist permission from creator
3. Run as dark posts through Meta/TikTok ads manager
4. Test 3-5 hook variations from the organic winners
5. Scale budget on the top 1-2 performers

**Targeting for Drift paid ads:**
- **Primary:** 22-38, interest in travel planning, Solo travel, Couples travel, Digital nomads, Trip planning
- **Lookalike:** Upload your existing users → build 1% LAL → scale to 2-5% LAL
- **Retargeting:** App installs who haven't subscribed → $8.99/mo upsell creative

**Meta campaign structure:**
- Campaign 1: App Installs — Broad (25-40, US/UK/AU/CA)
- Campaign 2: App Installs — Lookalike (1-3% of current users)
- Campaign 3: Retargeting — Free-to-Pro conversion (existing installs)
- Budget split: 60% broad / 30% LAL / 10% retargeting

## Appendix B: Renewal Scoring

Track every creator post against these criteria at 48h and 7 days post:

| Metric | Score: Renew | Score: Cut |
|---|---|---|
| Views (48h) | >50K | <20K |
| Views (7 days) | >100K | <40K |
| Comments | Real questions/engagement | Only emojis or <20 comments |
| Saves | >1% of views | <0.3% of views |
| App store mention | Any "what app is this" | None |

Creators above threshold get renewal offer within 72h of post. Don't wait — momentum matters.

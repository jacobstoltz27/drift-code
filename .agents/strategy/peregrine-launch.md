# Strategy — Launch Peregrine (Drift's AI travel companion)

*Produced by `brand-strategist` · source of truth for the whole campaign · goal: app installs + first "Ask Peregrine" activation.*

## 1. Positioning
For discerning travelers who want an extraordinary trip without the planning grind, **Peregrine** is the AI travel companion inside **Drift** that plans like a local who's already there — shaping the whole trip to your taste, budget, and crew. Unlike generic AI chat or an endless pile of blogs, Peregrine knows the non-touristy places, turns a question into a real itinerary in minutes, and never tries to sell you a booking.

*Why it's winnable:* named-persona AI + a social travel platform underneath it is a lane no generic chatbot or booking app occupies. The persona (a falcon that "already knows the way") makes the AI memorable and trustworthy in a category of faceless assistants.

## 2. Target audience (lead segment first)
- **Lead: "The Overwhelmed Dreamer"** — 24–38, aspirational, takes 1–3 big trips a year, saves travel reels constantly, but dreads the logistics. The trip matters emotionally; the planning is a chore.
- **Moment of need:** staring at 20 browser tabs, a blank itinerary, or a group chat that can't agree.
- **Secondary:** group-trip organizers (the friend who plans for everyone), couples planning the once-a-year big one, solo explorers who want "like a local."
- **Their words:** "I don't want the tourist stuff," "hidden gems," "someone just plan it," "trip of a lifetime," "like a local."

## 3. Emotional hook (everything ladders to this)
**"Land like you already know the place."** — the feeling that a city already belongs to you the moment you arrive.

## 4. Messaging pillars
1. **Ask, don't research.** Plan a whole trip in one conversation — *"Ask Peregrine."*
2. **Local instinct, not tourist lists.** A falcon's eye for the places guidebooks miss.
3. **Shaped to you.** Your taste, your budget, your crew — Peregrine fits the trip to all three.
4. **Idea → itinerary in minutes.** From "I want to go to Tokyo" to a real day-by-day plan, fast.

## 5. Objections → reframes
- *"Won't ChatGPT do this for free?"* → Peregrine is purpose-built for travel, current on real local spots, and outputs a structured itinerary that lives in Drift — one you can act on, tweak, and share. Not a wall of text.
- *"Is this just another travel blog?"* → It's a conversation, not an article. Personalized to you, not the same 10 tourist stops.
- *"Will it actually know the good, non-touristy places?"* → That's the whole point — falcon instinct for hidden gems, not top-10 lists.
- *"Is planning going to be work?"* → No. You ask; Peregrine plans.
- *"Do I have to pay?"* → No — asking Peregrine is core to Drift. (Drift Plus adds power features; it's the only paid upgrade.)

## 6. Differentiation
Only **Peregrine** is an AI companion that lives *inside a social travel platform* — it doesn't just answer, it builds itineraries that plug into Drift's feed, Trip Score, and your friends. A **named persona** (memorable, trustworthy) vs. faceless "AI." And categorically: **Drift is not a booking engine** — Peregrine plans the *experience*, it never sells inventory.

## 7. Launch angle
- **Lead (spearhead): "Ask Peregrine."** — the Siri-style verb. One line, instantly teaches the behavior. Pair with the falcon.
- **Backup angles to test:** (a) *"Land like a local."* (b) *"Your whole trip, planned in one conversation."* (c) *"The fastest way to the best places."* (falcon-speed).

## 8. Success signal
- **North-star (leading):** % of new installs who complete a **first Peregrine itinerary within 24h** (install → first "Ask Peregrine" → generated itinerary = activation).
- Supporting: cost per install (CPI), hook rate / thumbstop on the lead angle, D1 retention.
